Steve's Life in Parts

Pt. 1: St. Louis, MO

This is where it all started for Steve. 1986. Born and raised in St. Louis, Missouri he was an only child to Jane and Steve. He grew up in Affton, a part of South County and primarily attended St. George gradeschool. Went to SLUH, or St. Louis University Highschool where one of his favorite classes was Web Design, freshman year. Sophmore year Steve attempted to learn Java and it was TOO much with all the other classes. The class was dropped and unfortunately programming became an afterthought.

Pt. 2: College

Not wanting to be stuck in St. Louis or head to Mizzou, where everyone goes, Steve made a break for it. Decided to head to Lawrence, Kansas for 2 years at University of Kansas and eventually transfer to Long Beach, California to attend CSULB (California State University Long Beach). As he worked towards his Bachelor of Arts degree, in those 4 years, Steve joined a fraternity, drove across the United States, interned at Jimmy Kimmel Live! and Cartoon Network.

Pt. 3: Reality

The intention was to get a job in the entertainment industry right out of college. Not the reality of Steve. Graduating in 2009 was not the best experience. Steve had to make the decision to head back home to St. Louis with hopes of making it out to California again in the future, to chase the dream. Living back home with Mom only lasted 3 months before heading to Kansas City to live up there with friends. Jobs included pizza delivery, manager, and eventually sales and compliance in a corporate setting. Unhappy, Steve headed back to St. Louis deciding to try and do a reset once more. 4 years passed with lots of video games, weed, and the best thing to come into his life, Bear, his dog and best bud.

Pt. 4: Back to STL

Steve's hope was to move back home with his father and not only help himself, but be there for his father. It was not where Steve needed to be. A depression set in about what the future held, but knowing he had Bear, he was able to get through the tough times of struggling to find the right job. Two and a half long years passed until things changed for the better.

Pt. 5: Square, LaunchCode, the Future

Spring 2017 I was hired at Square. Another corporate environment, but one I felt at home in. Customer Support was where I started and have worked my way to become a Chatbot Specialist. Not only that, but I was introduced to programming again with LaunchCode. 15 years have passed since I dropped programming in highschool. Now I am back at it and ready to see what the future holds.
